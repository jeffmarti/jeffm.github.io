Washington SWE Volumes

This application displays daily gridded snow water equivalent (SWE) estimates, expressed as acre-feet of water storage, for 71 HUC8 watersheds falling within Washington State, derived from the NOAA SNODAS model. The estimates are aggregated to provide an estimate of the volume of water stored in the snowpack for each watershed on any given day. The app allows users to explore current and historical SWE conditions, compare different dates, and examine how current conditions relate to the historical record.

Ensemble Instream Flow Forecasts vs WAC Regulations

These plots compare ensemble seasonal runoff forecasts from the Northwest River Forecast Center (NWRFC) to regulatory flows adopted by the Washington Department of Ecology. Instream flows adopted into rule are water rights held by the state for instream flow purposes. Being able to forecast where flows are likely to be below regulatory thresholds can help predict where communication and compliance will be necessary.

Yakima Basin Water Dashboard

This dashboard combines daily reservoir storage from USBR Hydromet with basin snowpack estimates derived from SNODAS data provided by the National Operational Hydrologic Remote Sensing Center (NOHRSC) and Climate Engine. Together these represent total constructed and natural water storage. A climate explorer allows exploration of historic storage and runoff data in the context of temperature and precipitation anomalies.

Center of Mass: Changes in Runoff Timing at USGS Gages

This application plots changes in the Center of Mass timing at USGS Streamflow Gages Across the Western United States. The center of mass (COM) is an indicator of hydrologic change. It represents the midpoint of annual runoff within a water year (October through September).
